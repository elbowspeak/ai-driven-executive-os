# Market Map

Map the landscape of an entire market category - players, positioning, funding, trends.

## Instructions

When the user runs `/market-map [category]`, create a comprehensive market landscape visualization and analysis.

## Usage

```
/market-map agentic AI security
/market-map data infrastructure observability
/market-map developer tools
/market-map fintech infrastructure
```

## Mapping Process

### 1. **Define Category Boundaries**
- What's included/excluded?
- Adjacent categories?
- How defined by market?

### 2. **Identify All Players**
- Leaders (mature, well-funded)
- Challengers (growing, competitive)
- Emerging (early but promising)
- Niche (specialized)

### 3. **Map Positioning**
- How do they differentiate?
- Customer segments served?
- Technical approaches?
- Pricing strategies?

### 4. **Analyze Trends**
- Market direction?
- Consolidation vs. fragmentation?
- Technology shifts?
- Customer behavior changes?

## Mapping Strategy

### **Research Sources**

**Company Discovery:**
- Crunchbase/PitchBook (funding data)
- Product Hunt (new entrants)
- Y Combinator batches (emerging)
- Industry analyst reports
- Conference exhibitor lists
- Google search "[category] startups"

**Positioning Research:**
- Company websites (messaging)
- Product pages (features)
- Pricing pages (segmentation)
- Blog posts (thought leadership)
- Case studies (customers)

**Market Intelligence:**
- Industry reports (Gartner, Forrester)
- VC thesis posts (firm perspectives)
- Founder interviews (podcasts)
- Conference presentations
- Reddit/HN discussions (sentiment)

### **Visual Mapping Techniques**

**2√ó2 Matrix:**
- Choose two axes (price/complexity, open/closed, technical/business)
- Plot companies
- Identify white space

**Competitive Set Circles:**
- Concentric circles: leaders (center), challengers, emerging (outer)
- Size = funding or market share
- Color = technical approach or segment

**Timeline View:**
- X-axis: Time (early stage ‚Üí mature)
- Y-axis: Traction/size
- Track how companies move

### **Pattern Recognition**

Look for:
- **Convergence**: Multiple companies solving same way
- **Divergence**: Fragmentation into niches
- **Gaps**: White space opportunities
- **Crowding**: Over-invested areas
- **Innovation**: Novel approaches emerging

## Important Notes

- Update quarterly (markets evolve fast)
- Be comprehensive (don't miss key players)
- Show differentiation (not just lists)
- Include reasoning (why positioned where)
- Make it visual (humans process visuals better)
- Share widely (increases value)
- Track changes over time (evolution matters)
- Validate with experts (don't rely only on desk research)
- Connect to portfolio (make it actionable)
- Consider both direct and adjacent competitors

## Tips for Great Market Maps

1. **Be specific**: "Developer tools" too broad, "AI code completion" better
2. **Show differentiation**: Not just lists, explain *how* they differ
3. **Include portfolio**: Always show where your companies fit
4. **Track over time**: Quarterly updates show evolution
5. **Make it visual**: Consider creating actual visual diagrams
6. **Validate**: Talk to founders, customers, investors in the space
7. **Stay current**: Set alerts, update when major events
8. **Share insights**: Maps are most valuable when shared
9. **Connect to action**: Always end with "so what?"
10. **Build on research**: Link to `/research` and `/competitive-analysis` outputs
