# Portfolio Highlight

Create compelling company spotlight content to showcase portfolio companies and build thought leadership.

## Instructions

When the user runs `/portfolio-highlight [company]`, generate polished content highlighting a portfolio company's journey, insights, or achievements.

## Usage

```
/portfolio-highlight Tenzai
/portfolio-highlight XORQ
/portfolio-highlight ConfigHub
/portfolio-highlight [any portfolio company]
```

## Purpose

Portfolio highlights serve multiple purposes:
- **Thought leadership:** Share insights from working with companies
- **Portfolio support:** Give companies visibility and credibility
- **Deal flow:** Demonstrate portfolio quality attracts more deals
- **LP communication:** Show portfolio progress and your value-add
- **Network effects:** Attract customers, partners, talent to companies
- **Personal brand:** Position yourself as valuable investor/operator

## Content Types

### **1. Founder Journey Spotlight**
Focus on the founders' story, decisions, and learnings.

**Use when:**
- Founder has interesting background or insight
- Company made pivotal decision worth sharing
- Lessons from journey are generalizable
- Celebrating milestone (funding, launch, customer)

**Structure:**
- Hook: Compelling moment or decision
- Context: Who they are, what they're building
- Challenge: What they faced
- Insight: What they learned (that others can apply)
- Update: Where they are now
- Your perspective: What you've learned working with them

### **2. Company Achievement Highlight**
Focus on a specific accomplishment or milestone.

**Use when:**
- Major product launch
- Significant customer win
- Funding announcement
- Industry recognition or award
- Impressive growth metric
- Technical breakthrough

**Structure:**
- Hook: The achievement
- Context: Why it matters
- Journey: How they got here
- Impact: What this enables
- Your perspective: What it signals about market/space
- Future: What's next

### **3. Market Insight Through Portfolio Lens**
Use portfolio company as case study for broader market insight.

**Use when:**
- Company validates emerging trend
- Their approach represents new category
- Their customer behavior signals market shift
- Competitive dynamics illustrate market evolution
- Technical approach demonstrates new paradigm

**Structure:**
- Hook: Market observation or trend
- Example: Portfolio company as case study
- Analysis: What this tells us about market
- Implications: What others should know
- Broader context: How this fits larger trends
- Your perspective: Investment thesis validation

### **4. Problem-Solution Narrative**
Focus on the problem they're solving and their unique approach.

**Use when:**
- Problem is underappreciated or misunderstood
- Their solution is novel or counterintuitive
- Customer pain point is universal
- Approach challenges conventional wisdom
- Educational value for audience

**Structure:**
- Hook: The problem (make it tangible)
- Status quo: How it's currently solved (inadequately)
- Insight: Why conventional approaches fail
- Solution: Their different approach
- Evidence: Traction or validation
- Your perspective: Why you bet on this approach

### **5. "Working With" Reflection**
Focus on your experience working with the company and what you've learned.

**Use when:**
- Celebrating anniversary (1 year, 2 years)
- Reflecting on partnership
- Sharing operational insights
- Demonstrating your value-add
- Building authentic relationship narrative

**Structure:**
- Hook: Unexpected learning or moment
- Context: When you invested, why
- Journey: What's happened since
- Learnings: What working with them taught you
- Value exchange: What you've hopefully provided
- Appreciation: Authentic recognition of their work

## Analysis Process

### 1. **Gather Information**

**From Vault:**
- Read all notes in `Biz Dev/Encoded Portfolio Companies/[Company]/`
- Review any meeting notes with founders
- Check for updates, milestones, challenges documented
- Look for compelling quotes or moments

**Recent Updates:**
- Check company website/blog for recent posts
- LinkedIn activity from founders
- Any press mentions or announcements
- Product releases or customer wins
- GitHub activity (if technical company)

**Your Relationship:**
- When did you invest?
- What's your involvement level?
- What value have you added?
- What have you learned from them?
- Any specific stories or moments?

### 2. **Find the Angle**

**What's compelling here?**
- Recent milestone worth celebrating?
- Insight worth sharing?
- Problem they're solving that resonates?
- Founder story that inspires?
- Market validation moment?

**What's authentic?**
- What genuinely excites you about this company?
- What have you actually learned working with them?
- What's non-obvious that you can share?
- What would you tell a friend about them?

**What's valuable to audience?**
- What can readers learn?
- What insight haven't they heard before?
- What makes them think differently?
- What helps them with their work?

### 3. **Check Company Preferences**

**Before publishing:**
- Does company want visibility right now? (Some prefer stealth)
- Any confidential info to avoid?
- Would they appreciate review/approval?
- Can you tag them? Use their content?
- Timing considerations? (coordinate with their announcements)

## Writing Guidelines

### **Authentic > Promotional**

**Good - Authentic:**
"Working with Tenzai has taught me that the best founders aren't afraid to challenge their investors' assumptions. When I suggested they focus on enterprise first, they showed me data proving developer-led growth was the right move. They were right."

**Avoid - Promotional:**
"So excited to announce our portfolio company Tenzai is crushing it! Best team ever! ðŸš€ðŸ”¥ So lucky to work with them!"

### **Specific > Generic**

**Good - Specific:**
"ConfigHub went from 50 to 500 customers in 8 months by doing one counterintuitive thing: they stopped trying to be everything to everyone and focused exclusively on configuration management for microservices."

**Avoid - Generic:**
"ConfigHub is growing fast by focusing on their customers and building a great product."

### **Insight > Update**

**Good - Insight:**
"Neurometric's journey illustrates a pattern I'm seeing across AI companies: the ones winning aren't the ones with the best models, they're the ones with the best data pipelines and operational discipline."

**Avoid - Update:**
"Happy to share that Neurometric just raised their Series A! Congrats to the team on this milestone!"

### **Show Value-Add > Claim It**

**Good - Show:**
"When DataFlint was deciding between usage-based and seat-based pricing, we built financial models for both scenarios. The data showed usage-based would unlock 3x expansion revenue. Six months later, that prediction is holding true."

**Avoid - Claim:**
"We provide tremendous value to our portfolio companies through strategic guidance and hands-on support."

## Content Calendar Strategy

### **Frequency:**
- Highlight 1-2 portfolio companies per month
- Rotate through portfolio (don't over-feature any one company)
- Align with their milestones when possible
- Mix content types (not all achievement posts)

### **Rotation:**
Track in `Content/Portfolio_Highlight_Schedule.md`:
```markdown
## 2025 Highlights

**Q4:**
```

## Integration with Portfolio Management

### **After each board meeting:**
Consider if there's:
- Achievement worth highlighting
- Insight worth sharing
- Milestone to celebrate
- Lesson to document

### **Monthly portfolio review:**
Run `/portfolio-update` and identify:
- Which companies have momentum
- Which need visibility boost
- Which have recent wins to celebrate
- Which provide market insights

### **Coordinate with founders:**
- Ask: "Any milestones coming up worth highlighting?"
- Offer: "Would you like visibility on X achievement?"
- Collaborate: "Let's co-create content around Y"

## Value to Portfolio Companies

**Direct value:**
- Visibility to potential customers
- Credibility (investor endorsement)
- Talent attraction
- Partnership opportunities
- Press attention (reporters follow investors)

**Network effects:**
- Your audience becomes their audience
- Warm intros from engagement
- Cross-portfolio connections
- Investor/founder community awareness

**Morale boost:**
- Recognition for hard work
- External validation
- Team motivation
- Recruitment tool ("Our investor wrote this about us")

## Important Notes

- **Celebrate, don't brag:** Make it about them, not you
- **Be genuine:** Only highlight what you truly believe
- **Protect confidentiality:** Get approval for sensitive info
- **Show don't tell:** Specific examples > vague praise
- **Add insight:** What can readers learn beyond "company is great"?
- **Time it well:** Coordinate with company's announcements
- **Engage deeply:** Respond to comments, facilitate intros
- **Track impact:** Did this actually help the company?
- **Rotate fairly:** Don't over-feature favorite companies
- **Stay authentic:** Your voice, your real observations

## Tips for Great Portfolio Highlights

1. **Make it about insight, not ego:** Readers should learn something
2. **Be specific with examples:** "40% QoQ growth" > "growing fast"
3. **Tell a story:** Journey, challenge, solution more engaging than facts
4. **Show your value-add:** Demonstrate, don't claim
5. **Rotate portfolio:** Every company deserves spotlight
6. **Time it strategically:** Align with company's goals and timing
7. **Collaborate with founders:** Make them co-creators
8. **Track what works:** Learn from engagement patterns
9. **Be authentic:** False enthusiasm is obvious and off-putting
10. **End with value:** What should reader remember or do?
