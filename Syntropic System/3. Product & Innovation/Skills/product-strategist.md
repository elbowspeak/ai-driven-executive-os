# Product Strategist

Strategic toolkit for Head of Product to drive vision, alignment, and organizational excellence.

## Core Capabilities

### 1. OKR Cascade Generation and Alignment
Create hierarchical objectives that cascade from company to product to team level.

#### OKR Cascade Framework
```
Company OKRs (Annual/Quarterly)
         ↓
Product OKRs (Quarterly)
         ↓
Team OKRs (Quarterly)
         ↓
Individual OKRs (Quarterly)
```

#### Alignment Strategies

**Growth Strategy**:
- Company: Market expansion and revenue growth
- Product: User acquisition and activation
- Team: Feature adoption and engagement
- Individual: Specific feature delivery

**Retention Strategy**:
- Company: Customer lifetime value
- Product: User engagement and stickiness
- Team: Retention features and improvements
- Individual: Churn reduction initiatives

**Revenue Strategy**:
- Company: Revenue targets and monetization
- Product: Conversion and upsell
- Team: Pricing and packaging features
- Individual: Revenue-driving capabilities

**Innovation Strategy**:
- Company: Market differentiation
- Product: New capabilities and experiences
- Team: Experimental features
- Individual: Proof of concepts

**Operational Strategy**:
- Company: Efficiency and scalability
- Product: Technical excellence
- Team: Process improvements
- Individual: System optimizations

#### Alignment Tracking
- Calculate alignment scores for each OKR
- Track contribution percentages
- Identify gaps and misalignments
- Adjust quarterly based on progress

### 3. Product Vision and Strategy

#### Vision Development
**Elements of Strong Product Vision**:
- Aspirational: Where we want to be in 5-10 years
- Inspiring: Motivates teams and stakeholders
- Clear: Easy to understand and remember
- Customer-centric: Focused on customer value
- Differentiated: Unique market position

#### Strategy Frameworks

**North Star Metric**:
- Single metric that captures core value
- Leading indicator of success
- Aligns all teams
- Guides decision-making

**Product Strategy Canvas**:
```
Vision → Strategy → Roadmap → Features
  ↓         ↓          ↓          ↓
 Why      What       When       How
```

**Strategy Elements**:
- Target customers and segments
- Value proposition
- Key differentiators
- Go-to-market approach
- Success metrics

### 4. Team Scaling and Organizational Design

#### Product Team Structure

**Small Team (1-10 engineers)**:
- 1 Product Manager
- Cross-functional pod structure
- Direct collaboration

**Medium Team (10-50 engineers)**:
- 2-5 Product Managers
- Product Manager per squad
- Product Lead coordination

**Large Team (50+ engineers)**:
- Product organization hierarchy
- Group Product Managers
- Product VPs by business unit
- Platform/Infrastructure PMs

#### Organizational Patterns

**Feature Teams**:
- Organized around user-facing features
- End-to-end ownership
- Fast iteration

**Platform Teams**:
- Organized around technical capabilities
- Enable feature teams
- Long-term investments

**Customer Teams**:
- Organized around customer segments
- Deep customer knowledge
- Specialized solutions

**Journey Teams**:
- Organized around user journeys
- Holistic experience ownership
- Cross-functional optimization

#### Scaling Considerations
- Manager to PM ratio: 1:5-8
- PM to Engineer ratio: 1:8-12
- PM to Designer ratio: 3:1
- Specialization vs generalization balance

### 5. Metrics and KPI Definition

#### Product Metrics Hierarchy

**Business Metrics** (Company-level):
- Revenue, growth, profitability
- Market share
- Customer lifetime value
- Brand value

**Product Metrics** (Product-level):
- Activation rate
- Engagement metrics
- Retention cohorts
- Feature adoption
- NPS

**Feature Metrics** (Team-level):
- Usage frequency
- Completion rates
- Time to value
- Error rates
- Performance metrics

#### Metrics Framework

**AARRR (Pirate Metrics)**:
- Acquisition: How users find you
- Activation: First user experience
- Retention: Users coming back
- Revenue: Monetization
- Referral: User-driven growth

**HEART Framework**:
- Happiness: User satisfaction
- Engagement: Interaction level
- Adoption: New user growth
- Retention: Rate of return
- Task Success: Completion rates

## Strategic Planning Process

### Quarterly Planning Cycle

**Month 1: Discovery**:
- Market research and analysis
- Customer feedback synthesis
- Competitive intelligence
- Team retrospectives
- Data analysis

**Month 2: Strategy Development**:
- OKR cascade creation
- Roadmap prioritization
- Resource allocation
- Risk assessment
- Stakeholder alignment

**Month 3: Execution**:
- Initiative kickoff
- Progress tracking
- Course corrections
- Communication
- Learning capture

### Annual Planning

**Q4 Previous Year**:
- Company strategy alignment
- Budget planning
- Team planning
- Major initiative scoping

**Q1 Current Year**:
- Annual OKR finalization
- Resource allocation
- Roadmap communication
- Execution begins

## Success Indicators

✅ **Strategic Clarity**
- Vision and strategy well understood
- Strong OKR alignment (>80%)
- Clear prioritization framework
- Stakeholder buy-in

✅ **Execution Excellence**
- OKR achievement rate >70%
- Roadmap on track
- Team velocity stable
- Quality metrics met

✅ **Market Impact**
- Market share growing
- Customer satisfaction high
- Competitive position strong
- Innovation recognized

✅ **Team Health**
- Engagement scores high
- Retention strong
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Career growth paths clear

⚠️ Missing key metrics
⚠️ Competitive losses
⚠️ Customer churn increasing
⚠️ Slow decision making
⚠️ Siloed teams
